Steering Wheels

Logitech G29/G920

Best entry level wheel by far. High Quality, Reliable and Reasonably Priced. Also readily available in-store at reatailers such as Best Buy, Walmart, Target and Gamestop.

Compatible with PS4 and PC. Provides decent force feedback.

Includes Robust 3 Pedal set. Pedals are unmatched at this price point.

An optional 6 speed manual shifter is available, but not required.

Buy Here

Thrustmaster T150/T300

T150 - Currently priced lower than the Logitech Options. More widely available online. Provides higher quality force feedback than Logitech offerings at the cost of reliability.

Base version includes a cheaper plastic 2 pedal set. Brake pedal feel is not as good as Logitech Offerings

T150 Pro - Pro upgrade includes sturdier 3 pedal set.

T300RS/TMX Pro - A step above both the G29 and the T150 in feedback quality, wheel rims can be changed and upgraded. Includes higher quality 3 pedal set.

Buy Here

Fanatec CSL Elite

Highest quality plug and play option, very reliable. Wheel and pedal set are highly upgradable.

Not as beginner friendly

Direct Drive

Many different direct drive offerings exist on the market. This is the most expensive option, highest realism/feedback quality. If you are just getting started, ignore this option for the time being.

Choosing a wheel

Picking a steering wheel can be tough. The wheel is one of the most important parts of the sim rig, it is the tool you use to interact with the sim. Buying a wheel is the first step to getting started in sim racing. The easiest way to choose a wheel is to pick the best wheel you can afford. Every single force-feedback steering wheel on the market is adiquite for a beginner. Higher quality wheels are simply a luxury. Unlike real-world racing, in sim racing equipment does not make you a faster driver.

Next Steps

Assuming your PC meets the required specs, a steering wheel is the only thing you need to get started. At this point you can start your sim racing career. Every other piece of sim racing equipment is optional. I would recommend getting some races under your belt before considering upgrading your rig.
